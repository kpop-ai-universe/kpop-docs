## INTRO :
First of all, I’m not an expert in Rvc nor a programmer, so I’m just sharing what I know. These are the steps I use; they may vary from device to device and also depend on which idol you want to train the model with. With that warning out of the way, let’s continue!

## STEP 0:
**THE FORMULA**
Basically, there’s a formula to train a clean model:
No reverb / High-quality samples + Long dataset + Enough epochs = Clean model.
If you follow each part of this formula, you’ll get a good model.

## STEP 1: NO REVERB / HIGH-QUALITY SAMPLES
My secret to finding high-quality samples is looking for them on the Kpop Stems Discord server: 🔗 https://discord.gg/tXNpJfGNkv
There, you can find good stems. Search carefully, since you can use lead vocals, back vocals, and even ad-libs.

**IMPORTANT:** Make sure the samples don’t have other voices or ad-libs mixed in. This is a common (and serious) mistake, because if you train a model with multiple voices, the AI will make mistakes, take longer to train, and the final result will be lower quality.

Another thing to watch out for is reverb, since it will make your model sound bad. To remove it, I recommend Adobe Podcast: 🔗 https://podcast.adobe.com/en/enhance

## STEP 2: LONG DATASET
Try to collect your idol’s vocal lines from everywhere. The more minutes of dataset you have, the more realistic and better your model will sound. You can look for covers, live stages, demos, concerts, song lines, etc.

💡 **Tip:** If you’re not satisfied with the length of your dataset, you can duplicate it (basically upload it twice). To be honest, this is more of a superstition—there’s no solid proof it improves the model, but many people still do it.

## STEP 3: ENOUGH EPOCHS
Want a next-level model? This is the key: epochs, epochs, and more epochs. It’s the best solution. I recommend training for 350–500 epochs, depending on the length of your dataset:
- If it’s long, use around 300–350.
- If it’s short, use 350–500.

And that’s it! Those are the steps I follow to train my models. Again, I’m not an expert—this is just what works for me. I hope it helps you out! Byee~ ✨ .
